About

Pei Zhou

Associate Researcher, College of Computer Science, Sichuan University.

My research focuses on computer vision and pattern recognition, with an emphasis on three-dimensional imaging, object detection and recognition, and the application of deep learning to visual tasks. I have published 40+ papers in top-tier journals and conferences, and have participated in multiple National Natural Science Foundation projects. My work has practical impact in intelligent surveillance, industrial inspection, and medical image analysis.

I am also interested in multimodal learning, intelligent imaging systems, and the development of deployable perception algorithms for real-world scenarios.

Research

Research Interests

3D Imaging & Measurement

Binary encoding, phase demodulation, and fringe projection profilometry for high-precision three-dimensional surface reconstruction and microscopic shape measurement.

Computer Vision & Pattern Recognition

Object detection, surface defect detection, and multi-label classification using deep learning architectures including YOLO, U-Net, and graph neural networks.

Multimodal Learning & Clustering

Multimodal clustering with semantic alignment, dominant view mining, and knowledge distillation for cross-modal data understanding.

Intelligent Imaging Systems

Infrared-visible image fusion, single-pixel imaging, and adaptive speckle projection for high-dynamic-range and challenging environment imaging.
